Changunarayan Temple

Changunarayan is one of the oldest Hindu temples in Nepal, known for its exquisite wood and stone carvings.

Situated near Bhaktapur, Changunarayan is accessible by road. Explore the ancient temple, admire the carvings, and enjoy the scenic views from the hilltop location.

Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple)

Swayambhunath, or the Monkey Temple, is a sacred Buddhist site with a history dating back over 2,500 years. It represents the harmony between different religious communities.

Located on a hill in the Kathmandu Valley, Swayambhunath is accessible by road or on foot. Enjoy the panoramic view of the valley and explore the stupa, shrines, and surrounding monasteries.

Pashupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath is a sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It's an important pilgrimage site and holds cultural and religious significance.

Located in Kathmandu, Pashupatinath is accessible by road. Explore the temple complex, witness Hindu rituals, and visit the ghats along the Bagmati River.

Chitwan National Park (UNESCO World Heritage Site)

Chitwan National Park is crucial for the conservation of endangered species like the one-horned rhinoceros and Bengal tigers. It also showcases the harmonious coexistence of nature and the indigenous Tharu culture.

Reach Chitwan by road or air. Participate in jungle safaris, bird watching, and canoe rides. Visit Tharu villages to experience their traditional lifestyle. Lodges and resorts in the park offer various wildlife and cultural experiences.

Lumbini (Birthplace of Buddha - UNESCO World Heritage Site)

Lumbini is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautam, who later became Buddha. The site holds immense religious significance for Buddhists worldwide. The Maya Devi Temple and Ashoka Pillar are key attractions, along with monastic zones built by various countries.

How to Visit: Lumbini is accessible by air or road. Explore the sacred garden, visit the Maya Devi Temple, and see the Ashoka Pillar. Monasteries built by different countries provide a unique international perspective on Buddhism.

Boudhanath Stupa

Boudhanath is one of the largest stupas in Nepal, symbolizing Tibetan Buddhism. It is a center for meditation and prayer.

Situated in Kathmandu, Boudhanath is easily accessible. Walk around the stupa, spin prayer wheels, and visit monasteries. It's a serene place for reflection and spiritual exploration.

Sagarmatha National Park

Sagarmatha National Park is home to Mount Everest, the world's highest peak. It preserves unique ecosystems, including alpine meadows and Sherpa cultural heritage.

Fly to Lukla and trek to Everest Base Camp or explore the surrounding trails. Experience Sherpa hospitality in villages like Namche Bazaar. The park offers stunning views of Everest, Lhotse, and other peaks.

Kathmandu Durbar Square:

Kathmandu Durbar Square, also known as Basantapur Durbar Square, is the historic seat of the Nepalese royalty. It is surrounded by palaces, courtyards, and temples, showcasing a blend of Hindu and Buddhist architecture. The Kumari Ghar (Living Goddess's residence), Taleju Temple, and Kal Bhairav are notable landmarks.

Located in the heart of Kathmandu, the square is easily accessible by road. Visitors can explore on foot and enjoy the intricate carvings, architecture, and cultural heritage. The square is a central hub for festivals and events.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is renowned for its medieval architecture, well-preserved courtyards, and vibrant culture. The 55-Window Palace, Nyatapola Temple, Bhairab Nath Temple, and the Golden Gate are some of the highlights, showcasing the city's artistic and architectural heritage.

Bhaktapur is a short drive from Kathmandu. Explore the square on foot, marvel at the craftsmanship of the structures, and immerse yourself in the local culture. Bhaktapur is known for its traditional Newari cuisine, adding to the overall experience.

Patan Durbar Square

Patan Durbar Square, also known as Lalitpur Durbar Square, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The square features ancient palaces, temples, and shrines with intricate wood and metalwork. The Krishna Mandir, Hiranya Varna Mahavihar (Golden Temple), and the Patan Museum are key attractions.

Patan is adjacent to Kathmandu and easily accessible. Explore the square on foot, visit the museum for a deeper understanding of the art and history, and witness the vibrant atmosphere during traditional festivals.
