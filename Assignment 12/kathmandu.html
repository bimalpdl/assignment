Kathmandu Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square, also known as Hanuman Dhoka Durbar Square, is the beating heart of Nepal's capital city. This UNESCO World Heritage site isn't just a cluster of ancient buildings; it's a vibrant mosaic of history, culture, and daily life. Stepping into the square is like stepping into a time capsule, where centuries-old temples and palaces whisper tales of kings and gods, while artisans carve intricate woodwork and street vendors hawk colorful souvenirs.

Prepare to be awestruck by the intricate detailing of the Kasthamandap, a towering seven-story pagoda built entirely from wood, or marvel at the nine-story Nautale Durbar, a testament to the Shah dynasty's power. Hanuman Dhoka itself, meaning "Monkey Gate," holds the secret within its courtyards: the Kumari Ghar, residence of the Kumari, a young girl worshipped as the living embodiment of the Hindu goddess Durga. Be sure to catch a glimpse of her auspicious face appearing at a window during the daily puja.
