Changunarayan Temple

Changunarayan Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu located on a hilltop in the Changunarayan Municipality of Bhaktapur District, Nepal. It is one of the oldest temples in Nepal, dating back to the 4th century AD. The temple is a two-story pagoda-style structure made of brick and stone. It is richly decorated with intricate carvings of Hindu deities and mythology.

Changunarayan Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is a popular tourist destination and is considered to be one of the most important religious sites in Nepal. The temple is visited by Hindus from all over the world. The temple is open to the public from sunrise to sunset. Visitors must dress modestly and cover their shoulders and knees. No shoes are allowed inside the temple.
