Changunarayan Temple

This ancient Hindu temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is renowned for its intricate wood and stone carvings. Changunarayan Temple is one of the oldest temples in Nepal and is located on a hilltop near Bhaktapur.

Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple)

Perched atop a hill in the Kathmandu Valley, Swayambhunath is a sacred Buddhist stupa adorned with colorful prayer flags and inhabited by playful monkeys. The site offers panoramic views of the valley and is a symbol of harmony between the Buddhist and Hindu communities.

Pashupatinath Temple

Located on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu, Pashupatinath is a sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is a major pilgrimage site and hosts various festivals. The temple complex also includes cremation ghats and sacred shrines.

Chitwan National Park (UNESCO World Heritage Site)

Situated in the southern part of Nepal, Chitwan National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the best wildlife reserves in Asia. It is home to diverse flora and fauna, including the endangered one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tigers, elephants, and various bird species. Visitors can enjoy jungle safaris, elephant rides, and bird-watching tours.

Lumbini (Birthplace of Buddha - UNESCO World Heritage Site)

Lumbini is a significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists as it is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Buddha. The sacred garden of Lumbini houses ancient ruins, monasteries, and the Mayadevi Temple. It is a serene and peaceful place where visitors can learn about Buddhism and explore the spiritual heritage of Nepal.
